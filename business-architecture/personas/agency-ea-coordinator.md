# Persona: Agency EA Coordinator

**Validation Status: Assumed** — drafted without direct user research. Must not drive implementation until validated through interviews with real agency-level IT staff responsible for enterprise architecture in a state or local government context.

## Role Type
Internal — Agency IT / Line Department

## Who They Are
The Agency EA Coordinator is responsible for maintaining the enterprise architecture for a single government agency or department (e.g., Department of Motor Vehicles, Department of Health). They may hold a title like Enterprise Architect, IT Director, or Solutions Architect. They want to do real EA work — documenting capabilities, cataloguing applications, mapping to personas — but they operate within a broader government IT ecosystem and occasionally need to coordinate with central IT or peer agencies.

## Goals
- Maintain the agency's EA repository without needing help from central IT
- Connect agency capabilities to enterprise-wide capabilities when it makes sense, on their own terms
- Share appropriate content (capabilities, personas) with peer agencies facing similar problems
- Reference central IT's enterprise personas and capabilities as a starting point rather than rebuilding from scratch
- Demonstrate the agency's EA work to central IT, leadership, or auditors without exposing sensitive internal details

## Pain Points
- Central IT tools are built for the enterprise view and don't fit how agencies actually work
- No easy way to say "our permitting capability implements the enterprise 'Licensing & Permitting' capability"
- Sharing with peer agencies is ad hoc — email, meetings, or shared SharePoint folders
- Peer agencies are solving the same problems independently with no awareness of each other
- Any integration with central IT feels like reporting upward or losing control of their own data

## Critical Insight
The Agency EA Coordinator will adopt enterprise alignment only if it is opt-in and low-friction. They need to feel that connecting to central IT or peer agencies adds value to their own work — not just to someone else's reporting. The right framing is "browse and adopt what's useful, share what you're comfortable sharing" rather than "report your status to central IT."

## Relevant Capabilities
- Connecting the agency org to central IT and peer agencies
- Setting visibility on personas, capabilities, and applications
- Linking local capabilities to enterprise capabilities
- Browsing instance-wide and connected-org content
- Managing incoming cross-org link approval requests
